This project was born from a desire to create educational art and design content that will hopefully inspire others to find their own joy in participating in the deeply human activity of creativity.

Many people struggle to pick up a pencil or paint brush because they want to be good at it immediately. Or maybe they feel like they don’t have the time to learn a new skill and they think that whatever they are doing must be perfect on the first try because if it isn’t, then it’s “not worth doing.”

But I’d argue the act of creating is, itself, the purpose of the practice.

This project has been a work in progress for the entirety of my time in the MCAD MAGWD program, even though I didn't fully realize that at the time. I conducted a wide range of research over the last two years, but some of the most poignent points came from "The Creative Spark" by Agustin Fuentes, professor of anthropology at Princeton University.

With this understanding of the humanity behind creativity, I felt like I was ready to explore how I could help others to learn this information and feel safe to participate in creative activities.

I proceeded with designing a brand that could be used to package these educational videos I wanted to make. I spent a lot of time developing the brand's tone, color, type, logo, and visual imagery. The complete details of my design process can be found in my Progress Platform.
